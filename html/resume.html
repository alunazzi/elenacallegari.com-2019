I am a comparative syntactician, a freelance speech-to-text consultant and an overall language expert. I study how different communicative goals influence the way sentences are phrased and the way a conversation develops (information structure). I also try to account for why different languages differ in the way they arrange words (comparative syntax & word order variation). I hold several degrees in linguistics from various European universities and an honorary affiliation at University College London (UCL). I have worked on Bulgarian, Macedonian, Bosnian-Serbian-Croatian, Russian, Italian, French, Spanish, Norwegian, Dutch and Bambara.
